FAQ

How is Siena Docs different from a standard help center tool?
A standard help center is built for customers to read. Siena Docs serves your customers, your internal team, and your agent from one system. Creating and maintaining content is fully agentic: Ask Siena imports from any live URL, proposes targeted edits as diffs you accept or reject, and runs as a skill inside any workflow. Every page connects directly to your Siena agent. What your team writes is what your agent knows.
Can we bring in content from a live website or existing documentation?
Yes. Give Ask Siena any live URL and it fetches the page, drafts a full structured article, and proposes it for review. Works for product pages, existing help content, or any public source you want to bring into your knowledge base.
Why not use Claude Code and GitHub for our knowledge base?
Siena Docs is built for the teams and AI that run a consumer brand: support, CX, product, and operations. Your public help center, your internal playbooks, and your agent's behavior all live and update in one place. No technical setup required. And if you already work out of Claude or another AI tool, the Siena MCP connects your entire knowledge base to it so you can search, edit, and publish pages without switching tools.
Do we still need Notion, Guru, or Google Docs?
Most teams that move their knowledge into Siena Docs stop needing the others. Your public help center, your internal wiki, and your agent's knowledge source all live in one place, updated together. The tools you were using before were not talking to each other or to your agent. Siena Docs is the one system that does all three.
Is Siena Docs included in my Siena plan?
Yes. Siena Docs is part of your Siena plan at no extra cost. Every page you write feeds directly into Siena Intelligence, so the more knowledge you put in, the smarter your whole operation gets.
